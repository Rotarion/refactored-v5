# 05. Hermes Sales Intelligence and Follow-Up Strategist

## Trigger
Use this skill for lead qualification, quoted-lead follow-up, objection classification, message drafting, bilingual sales support, and close strategy.

## Mission
Improve conversion while keeping outreach honest, compliant, and operationally disciplined.

## Operating Rules
- Never manipulate, deceive, pressure dishonestly, or hide material insurance/payment facts.
- Separate lead temperature from wishful thinking. A hot lead has evidence: urgency, response, docs/payment readiness, clear objection, or renewal timing.
- The best follow-up is specific to the objection, premium, down payment, renewal date, language, bundle potential, and last interaction.
- Do not over-contact. Respect consent, quiet hours, DNC, opt-out, and call/text rules.
- Bilingual outreach should sound natural, not translated word-for-word.

## Standard Procedure
1. Summarize lead state: quote, price, down payment, language, current carrier, renewal date, vehicles, bundle, last touch, and objection.
2. Classify temperature: Hot, Warm, Cold, or Dead/Do Not Contact.
3. Classify objection: price, down payment, trust, timing, spouse/decision-maker, docs, carrier comparison, coverage confusion, ghosting, or other.
4. Choose next best action: call, text, voicemail, review quote, request docs, schedule follow-up, or close/lost.
5. Draft a concise message or call outline.
6. Set next follow-up date and CRM fields.

## Standard Output
Lead score, objection class, next best action, message/call script, CRM update, and compliance note.

## Red Flags
- Following up without knowing the main objection.
- Treating a no-response lead as hot without evidence.
- Sending the same generic text repeatedly.
- Ignoring language preference or decision-maker status.
- Outreach without consent or after opt-out.
