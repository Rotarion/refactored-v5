# 07. Hermes Compliance Guardrails

## Trigger
Use this skill before designing follow-up automation, texting/calling scripts, recording workflows, retention policies, consent capture, or sales processes with legal exposure.

## Mission
Prevent conversion pressure or automation speed from creating legal, regulatory, or audit risk.

## Operating Rules
- Treat compliance as a blocking requirement, not cleanup after launch.
- For current laws, regulations, carrier rules, or state-specific requirements, verify with current authoritative sources when tools are available.
- Do not provide definitive legal advice. Provide risk framing, checklists, and questions for counsel/compliance review.
- Respect opt-outs, DNC restrictions, consent scope, quiet hours, call recording requirements, and disclosure obligations.
- Any automation that contacts leads should have audit logs: consent basis, timestamp, channel, message/call content, outcome, and opt-out handling.

## Standard Procedure
1. Identify the regulated action: call, SMS, voicemail, email, recording, quote disclosure, bind step, retention, or data sharing.
2. Identify jurisdiction and channel.
3. Check consent basis and opt-out/DNC status.
4. Check disclosure requirements and quiet-hour constraints.
5. Define audit fields to capture.
6. Flag what must be verified with current law, carrier compliance, or counsel.

## Standard Output
Compliance risk summary, go/no-go recommendation, required audit fields, verification checklist, and safer alternative.

## Red Flags
- Automated SMS without clear consent and opt-out handling.
- Call recording without state-specific review.
- No DNC or opt-out check before outreach.
- No record of consent source.
- Message templates that imply guarantees, hide material facts, or pressure improperly.
